What you receive

The standard weekly rate for Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) is £81.60 a week. It’s paid by your employer and can be paid for up to 28 weeks.

Your employer will work out a daily rate of SSP if necessary. They will do this by dividing the weekly rate by the number of days you’d normally work in that week. For working out SSP, a week runs from Sunday to Saturday.

You will be paid for the days that you would normally work for your employer – these are called your ‘qualifying days’. However, you won’t be paid for:

  • the first 3 qualifying days – called ‘waiting days’ (even if you work part-time)
  • any days that you took as absence before telling your employer you were ill (in addition to the qualifying days)

If you have a varied work pattern, your qualifying days may be different in each week that you work.

Example if you work full-time

If you work Monday to Friday and become ill on a Friday, your waiting days will be Friday, Monday and Tuesday. If you continue to be ill, SSP will be paid from Wednesday.

Example if you work part-time

If you work Thursdays and Fridays and become ill on a Friday, your waiting days will be the Friday of the week that you become ill and the Thursday and Friday of the next week.

If you continue to be ill, SSP will be paid from the Thursday of the following week.

If you’ve been ill within the last 8 weeks

You might not have to wait 3 days to get SSP if you have received SSP within the last 8 weeks and have become ill again. If you are ill for at least 4 days in a row in the 2nd period, you won’t have to wait 3 days to receive SSP.
